Field briefing

Built for operators turning Chicago data into action

Chicago's Money started as a Bronzeville spreadsheet for community organizers. It now powers outreach teams, lenders, and journalists who need transparent salary, permit, and budget intelligence while they move across the city.

35,847 Payroll rows tracked

Nightly syncs to the official Socrata dataset with rank, department, and compensation context.

11 feeds Data pipelines

Permits, business registrations, inspections, FOIA releases, and Council agendas.

4 minutes Average lead prep

Mobile templates let reps brief a neighborhood before a meeting or canvass.

How we work the open-data stack

Data plumbing Direct Socrata pulls

We ingest payroll, permits, inspections, and Council votes directly from the City of Chicago and Cook County datasets.

QA & context Editorial checkpoints

Data journalists annotate anomalies, overtime spikes, and policy changes so mobile readers see what matters.

Delivery Tap-first packaging

Every feed is compressed into cards, swipable streams, or SMS-ready recaps for field usage.

Core tools: PostgreSQL materialized views, serverless fetchers, and a mobile-optimized CDN cache keep payloads light and current.

Questions we hear the most

How fast do salary updates publish?

Employment changes sync within hours of hitting the city portal. Nightly automation confirms new hires, retirements, and overtime adjustments.

Can we request custom views?

Yes. Agencies and civic partners can commission ward, department, or compliance views delivered via daily SMS or Slack webhooks.

Do you cover Cook County or State of Illinois data?

Cook County payroll is in beta with similar mobile packaging. State-level feeds are available for enterprise partners; ask for access in the contact form.
